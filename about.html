WHAT IS AYURVEDHA?

Ayurveda, a natural system of medicine, originated in India more than 3,000 years ago. The term Ayurveda is derived from the Sanskrit words ayur (life) and veda (science or knowledge). Thus, Ayurveda translates to knowledge of life.

Ayurvedic medicine is about much more than providing treatment. Ayurveda is one of the worldâ€™s oldest medical systems. Originating in India 5,000 years ago, it relies on a natural and holistic approach to physical, mental and emotional health.

WHY AYURVEDHA?

Ayurvedic practitioners assess an individual's constitution (dosha) and specific health concerns to tailor treatment plans and select the most suitable Ayurvedic pills.

Ayurvedic pills typically contain natural ingredients, including herbs and other plant-based substances. Many people prefer natural remedies because they are perceived as having fewer side effects compared to synthetic drugs.

Ayurvedic pills and supplements are used not only for treating existing health problems but also for preventive care. They can help strengthen the body's natural defenses and reduce the risk of developing certain diseases.

Some Ayurvedic pills are used to manage chronic conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, and asthma, often in conjunction with conventional treatments.

Ayurvedic approaches are often considered safer during pregnancy and lactation, offering natural remedies for common discomforts experienced during these periods.

Certain Ayurvedic herbs are believed to boost the immune system, which can be especially valuable during times of increased susceptibility to illness.
